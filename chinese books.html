Recommended Books

By reading interesting content in the language you want to learn — content that grabs and holds your attention — you can pick up all the vocabulary and grammar you need to know. When you read interesting content in meaningful chunks, your brain naturally absorbs word meanings and grammar rules.

1. 老夫子 Old Master Q - 王澤 Alfonso Wong

Reading Level: Easy | Genre: Gag-a-Day Comic

Old Master Q is a classic comic strip created by Alfonso Wong in the early 1960's in Hong Kong and has been a huge hit across Asia since then. Following the lives of Old Master Q, Big Potato, and Mr. Chin, the comics centres around humour, with characters usually portrayed in a variety of social statuses, professions and time periods, ranging from beggars and office workers to actors and ancient warriors.

2. 長歌行 Song of the Long March - 夏达 Xia Da

Reading Level: Easy | Genre: Adventure Comic

This manhua spans 61 chapters and follows the story of a young princess. After her family is killed during a coup, she dresses as a boy and assumes a new identity as a military strategist in order to exact revenge on those that destroyed her family. It has brief dialogue, so consider this a good manhua for beginner learners.

3. 丁庄梦 Dream of Ding Village - 阎连科 Yan Lianke

Reading Level: Intermediate | Genre: Contemporary Fiction

Based on a true story, this novel depicts the harsh reality that unfolded in China after the AIDS epidemic ravaged a small town as a result of blood-selling businesses. The story is told through the voice of a dead child—his father one of the most dangerous blood buyers. His grandfather pained by the loss of his grandson and ashamed by his own son’s part in his death.

4. 西遊記 Journey to the West - 吳承恩 Wu Cheng'en

Reading Level: Intermediate | Genre: Classic Fanatasy Fiction

Consisting of 100 chapters, this fantasy relates the adventures of a Tang Dynasty priest Sanzang and his three disciples, Monkey, Pig and Friar Sand, as they travel west in search of Buddhist Sutra. It is regarded as one of the greatest Classic Chinese Novels, and has been described as arguably the most popular literary work in East Asia.

5. 撒哈拉的故事 The Stories of Sahara - 三毛 San Mao

Reading Level: Advanced | Genre: Autobiographical Travel Literature

"Stories of the Sahara" is San Mao's first published collection of stories. Part travelogue, part memoir and a valentine to her love with her husband in a foreign land, it quickly established Sanmao as a travel writer with a unique voice and perspective. It was met with immediate success, and reprinted four times within a month and a half of its first print-run.

6. 三國演義 Romance of the Three Kingdom - 羅貫中 Luo Guanzhong

Reading Level: Advanced | Genre: Historical War Fiction

The story – part historical and part fictional – romanticises and dramatises the lives of feudal lords and their retainers, who tried to replace the dwindling Han dynasty or restore it. While the novel follows hundreds of characters, the focus is mainly on the three power blocs that emerged from the remnants of the Han dynasty.
